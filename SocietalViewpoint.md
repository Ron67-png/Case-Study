# __Defining the Societal Viewpoint__
The societal perspective examines how the development of the internet has influenced communities, education, communication, access to information, and overall quality of life. This viewpoont focuses on how different groups benefit or are disadvantaged by this technological evolution.

# __Educational & Knowledge Impact__
* ### Positive - Increased Access to Information:
The internet provides instant access to information on virtually any topic, giving people new learning opportunities, self-education and digital literacy.

* ### Positive - Online Learning Growth:
* The internet has made it easier and faster to gather information for students to access courses, tutorials, and educational platforms regardless of location.   

### Negative - Information Overload:
* The vast amount of information online can overwhelm users and make it difficult to identify credible sources.

### __Communication & Social Connection__
### Positive - Global Connectitvity:
* Social media platforms allow people to connect,
share experiences, and participate in social
movements, potentially fostering a sense of
community.

### Positive - New Social Communities:
* Online platforms give people spaces to find support, share interests, and express themselves.

### Negative - Social Isolation
* While social media can connect people, it can also
lead to social isolation and a decline in face-to-face
interactions, potentially impacting mental health.

### Negative - Cyberbullying & Harassment
* The internet has created new spaces for harmful behaviour thst affects mental health.

# __Social Equity Concerns__
### __Digital Divide__
* Not everyone has equal accesss to reliable internet, creating inequality in education, work, and communication.

### __Geographic Disparities__
* Rural areas often face slower speeds, limited network coverage, or higher costs.

### __Accessibility Issues__
* While improving, not all online services support users with disabilities or multiple languages.

# __Specific Societal Evidence__
* Over 400 million students received free or digital access during COVID 19
* Rural communities still struggle with slow or inconsistent internet.
* Theres also evidence that shows accessibility tools such as real-time captions and screen readers have improved the online experience for many users.
